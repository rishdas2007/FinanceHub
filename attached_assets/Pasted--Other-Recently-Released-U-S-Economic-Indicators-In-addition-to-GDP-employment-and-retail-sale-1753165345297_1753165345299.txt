## Other Recently Released U.S. Economic Indicators

In addition to GDP, employment, and retail sales, several other major economic indicators were released in July 2025. Highlighting these provides a more comprehensive snapshot of current conditions.

### 1. Consumer Price Index (CPI) / Inflation

- **Latest Data:** The annual inflation rate rose to 2.7% in June 2025, up from 2.4% in May. Core inflation (excluding food and energy) increased 2.9% over the year, slightly below forecasts.
- **Monthly Change:** CPI rose by 0.3% in June, the largest increase in five months.
- **Release Date:** July 15, 2025[1][2][3].

### 2. Consumer Confidence / Sentiment

- **Index Level:** The University of Michigan consumer sentiment index rose to 61.8 in July, a five-month high and above market expectations.
- **Inflation Expectations:** One-year inflation expectations fell to 4.4%, and five-year to 3.6%.
- **Release Date:** July 18, 2025[4][5][6][7].

### 3. Leading Economic Indicators

- **Conference Board LEI:** The Leading Economic Index (LEI) declined 0.3% in June 2025 and has dropped 2.8% over the first half of the year. This continued decrease signals slower economic growth ahead, though not an imminent recession according to the Conference Board.
- **Release Date:** July 21, 2025[8][9].

### 4. Industrial Production

- **Trend:** Industrial production and manufacturing output have shown improvement, with manufacturing output up 1.8% in the first five months of 2025.
- **Release:** Julyâ€™s data referenced performance through June 2025[10].

### 5. Housing Starts and Building Permits

- **Latest Movement:** Housing starts and building permits increased in June, both coming in above market expectations, reflecting some resilience in the housing sector[10][11].

### 6. Personal Income and Outlays

- **Personal Income:** For May 2025, personal income decreased by 0.4% monthly. Disposable personal income decreased by 0.6%, and personal consumption expenditures fell by 0.1%.
- **Release Date:** Data for June expected late July[12].

### 7. Initial Jobless Claims

- **Trend:** Initial claims for unemployment benefits continued to decline, falling for the fifth straight week as of the week ending July 18, 2025, confirming a strong labor market backdrop[10].
