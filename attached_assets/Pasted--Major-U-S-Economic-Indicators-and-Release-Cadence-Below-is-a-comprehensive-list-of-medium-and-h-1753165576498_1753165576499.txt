# Major U.S. Economic Indicators and Release Cadence

Below is a comprehensive list of medium- and high-importance economic indicators for the United States, along with their typical release schedules. These indicators are published by various government agencies and are crucial to understanding the nation's economic health.

## 1. Gross Domestic Product (GDP)
- **What it measures:** The total market value of goods and services produced.
- **Release cadence:** Quarterly (Advance, Second, and Third estimatesâ€”each about a month apart after the quarter ends), with annual revisions each summer.
- **Annual summary:** Highlighted in the annual U.S. Economic Report and BEA's yearly report[1][2][3][4].

## 2. Consumer Price Index (CPI)
- **What it measures:** Changes in prices paid by consumers (inflation).
- **Release cadence:** Monthly, with annual averages highlighted each January for the prior year[5][6].

## 3. Producer Price Index (PPI)
- **What it measures:** Changes in wholesale prices.
- **Release cadence:** Monthly, with annual averages published in summary tables[5][7].

## 4. Personal Income and Outlays
- **What it measures:** Personal income, spending, and the Federal Reserve's preferred inflation metric (PCE Price Index).
- **Release cadence:** Monthly, with annual totals and percent change data published in BEA's annual reports[2][3].

## 5. Employment Reports
- **Employment Situation (Nonfarm Payrolls, Unemployment Rate):** Monthly, with annual averages in January[8][5][9][6].
- **Job Openings and Labor Turnover Survey (JOLTS):** Monthly, annual summaries provided.

## 6. Industrial Production and Capacity Utilization
- **What it measures:** Output of factories, mines, and utilities; plant capacity usage.
- **Release cadence:** Monthly, with annual summary data in January[5][8].

## 7. Retail Sales
- **What it measures:** Total receipts by retailers.
- **Release cadence:** Monthly, annual totals reviewed each year[2][5].

## 8. International Trade Balance
- **What it measures:** Difference between exports and imports of goods/services.
- **Release cadence:** Monthly, with annual summaries from BEA and Census[3].

## 9. Housing Starts & Building Permits
- **What they measure:** New home construction and building permits.
- **Release cadence:** Monthly, with annual totals and year-over-year changes summarized[5][6].

## 10. Existing and New Home Sales
- **Release cadence:** Monthly, annualized rates and annual summaries provided[5][7].

## 11. Conference Board Leading Economic Index (LEI) & Coincident Index (CEI)
- **What they measure:** Composite indicators synthesizing multiple economic trends.
- **Release cadence:** Monthly, with rolling annual growth rates and summaries[10].

## 12. Consumer Confidence / Sentiment
- **Measured by:** Conference Board and University of Michigan.
- **Release cadence:** Monthly (University of Michigan also provides a preliminary estimate mid-month), with annual average index values[10][5].

## 13. Government Fiscal Reports
- **Federal Budget Balance:** Monthly (fiscal year ends September 30, with annual surplus or deficit reported).
- **Federal Reserve Monetary Policy Reports:** Semiannual economic overviews (February and July).

## 14. Additional Annual or Periodic Indicators
- **State and Local Government Finances:** Annually (Census Bureau).
- **Net Worth and Flow of Funds (Federal Reserve Z.1 Report):** Quarterly, with annual summaries each March.
- **National Income and Product Accounts (NIPA) Revisions:** Annually in July.

| Personal Income and Outlays                 | Monthly, Annual Summaries        | BEA                                        |
| Industrial Production                      | Monthly, Annual Summary          | Fed Reserve                                |
| Retail Sales                               | Monthly, Annual Summary          | Census/BEA                                 |
| Trade Balance                              | Monthly, Annual Summary          | Census/BEA                                 |
| Housing Starts & Permits                    | Monthly, Annual Summary          | Census                                     |
| Home Sales                                 | Monthly, Annualized/Annual Avg   | Census/NAR                                 |
| Consumer Confidence/Sentiment               | Monthly, Annual Average          | Conference Board/U. Michigan               |
| Leading/Coincident Economic Indices         | Monthly, Annual Summary          | Conference Board                           |
| Federal Budget                             | Monthly, Annual Fiscal Year End  | Treasury                                   |
| Monetary Policy (Fed Reports)              | Semiannual                       | Federal Reserve                            |

These indicators are at the core of economic analysis in the U.S. and are essential for understanding trends in growth, inflation, labor, consumption, housing, and government finance[5][3][6].
